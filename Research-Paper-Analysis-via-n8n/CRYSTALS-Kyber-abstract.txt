Title: CRYSTALS-Kyber (summary)
Source: https://pq-crystals.org/kyber/

Abstract (extracted/condensed):
CRYSTALS-Kyber is a module-lattice-based Key Encapsulation Mechanism (KEM) designed for post-quantum security. Based on the hardness of the Learning With Errors (LWE) problem in module lattices, Kyber provides efficient key generation, encapsulation, and decapsulation operations, and achieves IND-CCA security when combined with an appropriate transformation. Kyber is designed to offer a practical balance between security, performance, and compact key/ciphertext sizes, and it has been selected as a NIST Post-Quantum Cryptography finalist and later standardized candidate for general-purpose public-key encryption and key-establishment.
